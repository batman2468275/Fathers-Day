A letter, Father's Day 2026

For Baba.

with all my love, Zayd B. —
Sunday · June 21 · 2026
Scroll

There are eight things I want to say to you today — small things, big things, things I've been carrying around and never said out loud. So I wrote them down.

Eight things, in no particular order
01

Thank you for working hard for us.

I see it. The early mornings, the long days, the things you don't complain about. You carry weight quietly, and we have everything we have because of it. Thank you doesn't quite cover it — but thank you.

02

A house. Food on the table. Clothes on my back.

I know not everyone gets these things, and I never want to talk about them like they're guaranteed. They're not — they're because of you. A warm home, full meals, and the freedom to never worry about either. That's a kind of love that doesn't get said enough.

03

For being fun. For being kind.

You're the one who makes the room lighter when you walk into it. You laugh at your own jokes and that makes them funnier. And you're kind in the way that matters — to strangers, to family, to me even when I don't deserve it.

04

For taking me hunting — and everywhere else.

Hunting. Trips. The little adventures most people forget to make time for. You taught me how to be quiet outside, how to be patient, how to look closely at things. Those are the days I'll still remember when I'm old.

05

For the PC.

I know it's a "thing." But you knew how much it meant. You didn't just buy a machine — you backed something I love. The Ryzen, the GPU, all of it. Every time I sit down at it I'm reminded that you paid attention to what mattered to me.

06

For being there when I needed you most.

Not the easy times. The hard ones. The moments when I didn't have the words, or didn't want to say them, and you just showed up anyway. That's the part of being a father most people don't see. I see it.

07

For your time.

Time is the one thing you can't get more of, and you spend it on us anyway. Sitting with me. Listening. Driving me places. Answering questions you've answered a hundred times. Every minute you've given me, I've kept.

08

For being the best Baba in the world.

I know every kid says it about their dad. But I mean it. I really, genuinely, completely mean it. There's no one I'd trade you for. Not one person. You're it.

A poem I wrote for you

F·A·T·H·E·R

Far away, you drive every day Always going the same way Through challenges and pain, you always seem happy Holding your patience through our scrappy and crappy Extinguishing all negativity Raising us with the heart of empathy
— for you, Baba.
And finally

I love you, Baba.

— Zayd B.
